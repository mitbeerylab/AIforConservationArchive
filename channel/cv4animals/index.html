Hi All,

I am co-hosting a workshop/symposium at the upcoming World Seabird Conference in Hobart, Tasmania, September 7-11, 2026 that I think folks in this workspace would be interested in! This event is in-person only at the moment, but I am trying to look into the possibility of streaming presentations.

Workshop W02: Next-generation seabird monitoring: from technology and machine learning to conservation best practice
Conveners: Anna Vallery (Audubon Washington, USA), Wade Sedgwick (Island Foundation, USA), Matt Wood (University of Gloucestershire, UK), Alice Edney (National Trust Scotland, UK)
Emerging technologies are transforming how we monitor seabirds, providing critical insights into responses to threats such as climate change, habitat loss, and other environmental changes. Seabird monitoring programs around the globe are rapidly accumulating large datasets from drones, satellites, cameras, and other automated sensors, but refined protocols for managing and analyzing these datasets lag behind, limiting comparability and slowing adoption of new tools across programs and regions worldwide. This session will bring together experts in applications of these tools and machine learning in ecology to assess how technology can best support evidence-based management, sharing successes, pitfalls, and future plans while balancing innovation with realism about the limitations of technology in the field. The symposium will provide an opportunity for participants to learn about available tools and current work in this field. By bridging cutting-edge science and policy needs, the workshop will then provide an opportunity for those interested to collaboratively draft best-practice guidelines to ensure robustness, accessibility, reproducibility, and cost-effectiveness of next-generation seabird monitoring efforts, strengthening conservation planning while ensuring seabird monitoring remains robust into the next decade.

Abstracts for the conference are due January 15th! I am happy to answer any questions. Please feel free to pass along this information to anyone you think may be interested. I am also happy to chat with folks directly at annacvallery@gmail.com.

I hope to see some of you all there! https://worldseabirdunion.org/world-seabird-conference-home/
