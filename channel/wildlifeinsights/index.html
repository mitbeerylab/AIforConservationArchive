Sara Beery (sbeery@caltech.edu)
2019-11-14 13:44:03

*Thread Reply:* Totally! If you're not doing it already, I'd recommend taking the Microsoft MegaDetector (https://github.com/microsoft/CameraTraps/blob/master/megadetector.md) running it over your images, and keeping the high-confidence boxes, then performing "distillation" by assuming your image class label matches the box label and fine-tuning the detector with your set of classes. You can do that right away, no waiting for lawyers required 🙂

GitHub
microsoft/CameraTraps
Tools for training and running detectors and classifiers for wildlife images collected from motion-triggered cameras. - microsoft/CameraTraps
Original URL: https://github.com/microsoft/CameraTraps/blob/master/megadetector.md
Sara Beery (sbeery@caltech.edu)
2019-11-14 13:44:57

*Thread Reply:* Or you can use a chained approach, where you assume the detections are correct and train a cropped-box classifier (like what @Mathias Tobler has been doing)

Sara Beery (sbeery@caltech.edu)
2019-11-14 13:46:55

*Thread Reply:* We described this high-level approach in a short paper at the KDD Data Mining and AI for Conservation Workshop this year: https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.06772

arXiv.org

Biologists all over the world use camera traps to monitor biodiversity and wildlife population density. The computer vision community has been making strides towards automating the species...
